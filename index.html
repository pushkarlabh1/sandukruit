GOD OF SIGHT - Dr. Sanduk Ruit

The man who saved million of lives

For bringing the gift of sight, and productive life, to those most in need. Man in the middle of the image is Sanduk Ruit, an ophthalmologist (eye surgeon) from Nepal who has restored the sight of over 130,000 people across Africa and Asia using small-incision cataract surgery.

Here is the timeline of Sanduk Ruit life

  • 1954: Sanduk Ruit is born on September 4th in Olangchung Gola, Nepal.
  • 1978: Graduates with a medical degree from the Institute of Medicine in Nepal.
  • 1986: Completes his ophthalmology residency in Nepal and becomes a fellow of the Royal College of Surgeons in Edinburgh, UK.
  • 1987: Attends a workshop on intraocular lens implantation in Australia, sparking his interest in cataract surgery.
  • 1994: Co-founds the Tilganga Institute of Ophthalmology in Kathmandu, Nepal, with Dr. Geoff Tabin.
  • 1995: Performs the first successful small-incision cataract surgery (SICS) in Nepal, a technique he helped develop to make cataract surgery more accessible and affordable.
  • 1998: Launches the Nepal Eye Program, a partnership between the Tilganga Institute of Ophthalmology and the Fred Hollows Foundation, with the goal of eliminating avoidable blindness in Nepal
  • 2003: Receives the Ramon Magsaysay Award, often referred to as Asia's Nobel Prize, for his efforts to restore sight to Nepal's blind population.
  • 2008: Dr. Ruit performs the world's first cataract surgery with a technique called "tunnel incision" which reduces the need for stitches and accelerates the healing process.
  • 2011: Reaches a milestone of one million successful cataract surgeries performed in Nepal.
  • 2013: He introduces the "scleral-fixated intraocular lens implantation" technique to address complex cases of cataracts and intraocular lens dislocation.
  • 2014: Dr. Ruit expands his work beyond Nepal, collaborating with the Himalayan Cataract Project to provide sight-saving surgeries in other countries, such as Bhutan, Myanmar, and Ethiopia.
  • 2015: He is appointed an Honorary Officer of the Order of Australia in recognition of his outstanding contributions to restoring eyesight and training eye care professionals.
  • 2016: Awarded the National Order of Merit by the President of France for his contributions to ophthalmology and blindness prevention.
  • 2018: Dr. Ruit is awarded the prestigious Champalimaud Vision Award for his innovative and sustainable approach to eliminating preventable blindness in underserved communities.
  • 2019: His work is featured in the documentary film "Out of Darkness," which highlights his efforts to bring sight to people in remote areas.
  • 2020: Dr. Ruit's pioneering work and dedication to eradicating preventable blindness are recognized globally, and he continues to inspire and mentor the next generation of ophthalmologists.
  • 2021: Continues to work and make advancements in the field of ophthalmology, particularly in the areas of cataract surgery and blindness prevention.
